Why You’ll Love It

The OM2300S Zoom Stereo Microscope microscope is the holy grail for our industrial inspection customers. It features all the solid design features inspectors need, like:

 

 
  • Crisp and clean optics. The 7X-45X zoom range (6.4:1) is parfocal with dual diopters integrated into the oculars. The interpupillary adjustment range is 55mm-75mm.
  • A sturdy single-arm boom stand (with an included 84mm focus block). The pole is 17" tall with an effective usable height of 12". The horizontal arm extension is 20" including the microscope head.
  • A 4.5" working distance (which increases to 6.5" with the addition of 0.5x auxiliary Barlow lens). 
  • WF10X eyepieces and eye guards. , available in both binocular or trinocular format. 
  • Dual hand control. Rack and pinion focus includes controls for both your right and left hands to make focusing a lot more convenient. 

 

Say Goodbye to Weak Illumination

The OM2300S-JW11 comes with an 8-watt fluorescent ring light to give you a sharp and clear illumination to study your images under. You can upgrade to an 80 LED (or an even higher LED) ring light for shadow-free illumination.

 

It’s as Versatile as You Are

Besides its reliable workhorse build, the OM2300S-JW11 is versatile. Before you put it in your cart, you can customize:

  • The head type (binocular or trinocular)
  • The illumination type
  • The camera type

… to suit your application. 

 

 

You might be detecting product defects or failure analysis. You might be inspecting circuit boards or verifying measurements. Whatever the case, the OM2300S-JW11 allows you to pick your cake flavor, frosting, and sprinkles too. 

 

And after you’ve played around with the OM2300S-JW11 for a bit, you can customize it further with additional:

 

  • Barlow lens options (0.75x, 1.5x, or 2.0x)
  • Reticles
  • Eyepieces

 

… among other customizable options.

 

 

Swap Out that Hard Eyepiece for Soft Imaging

Do you spend hours hunched over the eyepiece of your microscope? Adding a digital microscope camera to your OM2300S-JW11 microscope reduces that neck pain and eye strain by showing you your images on a computer screen instead. That means you actually feel good about sitting back while you’re at work. 

When the beam-splitter lever is engaged in an OM2300S trinocular microscope, 80% of the light from the left optical path is directed to the camera port. The image can still be viewed through both eyepieces while the camera is in use, making this microscope more flexible than ever.
