7 easy ways to celebrate National Get Outdoors Day

Put down that phone. 

Turn off that tv. 

Shut that laptop down.

It’s National Get Outdoors Day, and these 7 great ideas are bound to make your day outside!

1. Become a birder for the day!

It’s a bird. It’s a plane. It’s…. Definitely a bird!

Fun, feathery and totally free! Birdwatching makes every day feel like a great safari! Just grab a pair of your Smithsonian’s Best Binoculars for Birding (and your exclusive Birdwatching Guide that comes with them!), and you’re ready to go.

2. Take a hike

Head out to your favorite trail, or better yet, take the road less traveled. Find a brand new path you haven’t explored before, and see where it leads! 

3. Take your lunch break in the park

Pack a picnic if you have the time, or just find a park bench, and spend your lunch hour in the warm sunshine, with the smell of fresh grass and the sound of birds chirping in the background. 

4. Fire up the barbecue 

Like you needed an excuse? Invite some friends or neighbors, and enjoy a good meal, with good company in the great outdoors!

5. Spend some quality thyme with your garden

A little garden therapy goes a long way!

Whether you’re tending to your peonies in your backyard, or growing tomatoes in a community vegetable garden, we encourage you to spend some quality time tending to your garden this week.  

6. Camp in your own backyard

Forget backcountry camping. Give backyard camping a try! You don’t have to go far to enjoy a good smore and an evening under the stars. Set up a private campsite in your very own backyard.
